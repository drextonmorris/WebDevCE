Who were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (1776) are remembered for their influence in American history, their bold actions, or their later roles in government. Here are a few of the most notable:

John Hancock (Massachusetts)
Famous for his large, stylish signature at the top of the document, so prominent that “put your John Hancock” became slang for a signature.
Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Main author of the Declaration and later the 3rd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
The oldest signer at age 70, renowned statesman, inventor, diplomat, and one of the most celebrated Founding Fathers.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
A leading advocate for independence, later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Organizer of colonial resistance (including the Boston Tea Party), known as a radical patriot.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only man to sign all four great U.S. founding documents: the Continental Association, the Declaration, the Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer, and the last surviving signer, living until 1832.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the resolution for independence in Congress.
Elbridge Gerry (Massachusetts)
Later Vice President; his name gave rise to the term “gerrymandering.”
